The Child Made of Glass

Food Allergy Awareness

The lunch table felt like a minefield.

Peanut butter sandwiches. Cookies shared between friends. Food passed across the table.

I learned to sit carefully. Watch carefully.

Even crumbs mattered.

Growing up →

Other kids shared snacks.

I carried an EpiPen.

Teachers learned my allergies. Friends learned to ask before offering food.

But I still felt different.
