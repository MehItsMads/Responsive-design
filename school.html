The Child Made of Glass

Food Allergy Awareness

The lunch table felt like a minefield.

Peanut butter sandwiches. Cookies shared between friends. Food passed across the table.

I learned to sit carefully. Watch carefully.

Even where I laid my arm on the lunch table mattered, that's why my mom was always with me during my younger years, she was my protector, she brought me everything I needed to not be left out.

Growing up →

Other kids shared snacks.

I carried an EpiPen.

Teachers learned my allergies. Friends learned to ask before offering food.

But I still felt different.

Birthday parties were the hardest.

Cake was never guaranteed.

My mom would always pack a "safe" cupcake So I wouldn't feel left out. And she was always there.

I remember pretending it didn't bother me, which at the time, It did, it was hard, but I dealt with it.
